2013
Arriving in Berlin
Landing in a new city and figuring out what to do with it. The underground music scene — Rampue, Solomun — becomes the compass. First real apartment, no furniture, no plan. Long walks. Clubs on weekday nights. Cinema as a social ritual. The era of discovering that a city can change you if you let it.

2014
Building a Home
First real DIY phase — lamp wiring, drilling, Baufassung. Making a Berlin apartment feel like somewhere. Bob Dylan becomes a permanent fixture on the speakers. Attack on Titan arrives and leaves a mark. Curious about everything, committed to nothing yet — but the restlessness is starting to look for direction.
🎵 Listening to
Bob Dylan · Eels · Eskmo · Sons of Perdition
🎬 Watching

2015
Movement & Creative Curiosity
Biodanza, contact improvisation, and Vicky Cristina Barcelona on repeat. DJ Shadow, Peter Fox, and early Google Analytics curiosity. Physical movement practices alongside a growing interest in film and data. A restless, exploratory year — no single obsession, many doors opening.
🎵 Listening to
Paul Simon · Sam Cooke · Bonobo · DJ Shadow
2016
Xavier Rudd & The Bowie Year
The peak listening year — 841 hours on Spotify. Xavier Rudd becomes an unlikely obsession: Spirit Bird, Follow the Sun on constant repeat. David Bowie dies in January and stays in the feed all year. Black Mirror lands like a gut punch. Animation resurfaces: The Secret of Kells reveals a whole world of studio craft that wasn't on the radar before.

The quietest music year of the decade — 472 hours, half of 2016 — but the quality is high. Otis Redding and The Beach Boys alongside Dylan. California Dreamin' becomes the most-played track. On screen: Rick and Morty earns a rare 10, Band of Brothers finally gets watched, and The Get Down briefly makes history feel electric.

Summertime becomes the year's most-played track. Future Man sneaks in as a 10. A year of comfort and consolidation rather than discovery.
